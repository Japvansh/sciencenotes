Physical & Chemical Properties

Physical changes are reversible, we deal with the physical properties of any substance

Physical changes are reversible, we deal with the physical properties of any substance (raw to cooked)

Physical changes are changes that alter the...

  1. Size
  2. Shape
  3. Location
  4. Physical state of a substance but not its chemical state


Paper ripped - physical (can be turned back into original)
Paper burned - Chemical (cannot be turned back into original)

Chemical changes are changes that alter the chemical make up of a substance

The new substance has a NEW SET of physical and chemical properties

When hydrogen and oxygen are mixed we get water, which is a liquid. Hydrogen and oxygen are gasses, so they are changed chemically into water.

Reactants in water: Hydrogen & Oxygen
Products in water: Water

Product is the final thing that cannot be changed.
Left side of the equation is always the reactants.

If you end up with a chemical or chemicals that you did not start with, It is a chemical change!
Eating of food is both physical and chemical (grinding = physical) (digesting = chemical)

Exothermic: Heat on outside
Endothermic: When heat is absorbed

Does the process of eating physically or chemically change the food?

Chewing changes food physically by breaking it down into smaller pieces.
Chemicals in the saliva begin to chemically change the food to make it easier to digest

Evidences of a chemical change:
  1. Creation of a gas (bubbles)
  2. Creation or loss of heat
  3. Colour change
  4. Form a precipitate (forming a solid substance out of liquid)
  5. Produces light
  6. Change in odor
  7. Irreversible

Physical changes can be reversed.
Chemical changes cannot be reversed and the atomic makeup has been transformed. They are transformed to totally different properties from the original

All matter has PROPERTIES (or characteristics) that describe its appearances and behaviours.

Observed with the senses
  • Colour: red, blue...
  • Odour: putrid, burnt, spicy...
  • Texture: coarse, smooth, fine, gritty…
  • Lustre: Shiny, dull...
  • Clarity: clear, cloudy, opaque…
  • Taste: sweet, salty, sour, bitter...


Some physical properties can be described with tests and measurements to aid your senses

State: solid, liquid, gas
Hardness: resistance to being scratched or dented
Malleability: ability to be bent into different shapes
Ductility: can be pulled into long thin wires
Melting and Boiling Points: temperature at which a substance changes state
Solubility: the ability of a substance to dissolve
Viscosity: how easily a liquid flows
Density: the amount of matter per unit volume of that matter

Describe the behaviour of a substance as it turns into a new substance
Combustibility – the ability of a substance to burn
Reactivity – how easily a substance reacts with another substance, like oxygen, acid or water
Sensitivity to Light –how the substance reacts when exposed to light
